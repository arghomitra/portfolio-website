Ostend Tour

Hey there, fellow explorers! 🌟 Today, I have an incredible tale of fun, laughter, and unexpected twists to share with you all. 🗺 My five friends and I decided to celebrate the EID festival by embarking on an unforgettable journey to Ostend, Belgium. 🇧🇪 Known for its mind-blowing sea beach and stunning views, we knew it would be the perfect getaway!

The Train Station Shenanigans

Our adventure began at Antwerp Central Train Station, where my friends smoothly bought their train tickets online. Being the savvy traveler that I am, I thought I might get a discount with my insurance paper, so I rushed to the office room to arrange a discount paper. After completing the process, I secured a ticket for around 11 euros. 🎫

The Race Against Time (and Laughter!)

As fate would have it, my friends were already waiting for me on the train, which was set to depart at 10:45 am. With just three minutes left, I sprinted like an Olympic runner and miraculously caught the train! 🏃‍♂ When I showed my discounted ticket, priced at 10 euros, my friends couldn't help but laugh heartily as they had bought youth discount tickets for just 7 euros. 😅 Lesson learned: check the terms before expecting discounts!

Cheap and Cheerful Brunch

After a scenic 2 and a half hour train ride, we finally reached Ostend train station. 🚉 We couldn't resist capturing some photos to commemorate our arrival in this picturesque coastal town. 📸 The delicious aroma from a nearby Turkish restaurant beckoned us, and with just a 4-minute walk, we indulged in a delightful 12 euro combo dish! It included a tantalizing dürüm (Turkish wrap), fries, and refreshing Coca-Cola. 😋

Sun, Sea, and Surprises

Fueled by our scrumptious brunch, we set out to explore the breathtaking Ostend sea beach. It was an extraordinary sight, especially with the sun shining brightly! 🌞 Two of our friends knew we were heading to a beach, but...

Discover Easy & Affordable Transport in Antwerp with DE LIJN!

🔹 In Antwerp, the bus and tram services are provided by DE LIJN. They cover the entire Flemish area, making it easy to get around.

🔹 They offer a convenient 10-time use bus ticket for just 17 euros, and you don't need any extra paperwork for this option. You can easily purchase it from any nearby day shop.

🔹 If you're a resident or have a long-term visa, you're in luck! You can grab a monthly pass for 33 euros. 📆

🔹 And the best part? You might be eligible for special discounts! With documents like insurance from CM, discount cards (like student or municipality cards), or VDAB registration, your yearly pass can cost you around 56 euros.

Here are some pricing details:

  • 📅 Age: 12 - 24 years
  • 🗓️ 1 month pass: €33
  • 📅 3 months pass: €81
  • 🗓️ 12 months pass: €215

📍 You can also visit their Antwerp office located at:

🏢 Pelikaanstraat, 2018 Antwerpen (Antwerpen-Centraal)
